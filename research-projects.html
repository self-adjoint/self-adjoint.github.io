Rolling Dice

A few (potentially) useful results about expected values of certain die-rolling problems. Suppose you roll a fair $n$-sided die (labeled $1,2,\ldots,n$) repeatedly until a certain event occurs (examples are given below). What is the expected number $E$ of rolls you would have to make?

  • Sum of rolls is at least $k$: $$ E(n,k) = \sum_{r=0}^{\lceil\frac{k}{n+1}-1\rceil} \binom{k-nr-1}{r}\left(-\frac{1}{n}\right)^r\left(1+\frac{1}{n}\right)^{k-(n+1)r-1} $$
  • Streak of $k$ rolls, i.e. $k$ consecutive rolls in strictly increasing order: $$ E(n,k) = \frac{1}{\displaystyle\sum_{r=0}^{n} \psi_{k,r}\binom{n}{r}n^{-r}} $$ $$ E(n,k) = \frac{1}{\displaystyle\sum_{r=1}^{k-1} \left(1-\omega_k^{-r}\right)\left(1+\omega_k^r z\right)^n} $$
  • Soft streak of $k$ rolls, i.e. $k$ consecutive rolls in non-decreasing order: $$ E(n,k) = \frac{1}{\displaystyle\sum_{r=0}^{n} \psi_{k,r}\mathcal{B}(n,k,r)n^{-r}} $$ $$ E(n,k) = \frac{1}{\displaystyle\sum_{r=1}^{k-1} \left(1-\omega_k^{-r}\right)\left(1-\omega_k^r z\right)^{-n}} $$

Notation

  • $\omega_k=e^{\tfrac{2\pi i}{k}}$ is the principal $k$th root of unity.
  • $\psi_{k,r}$ is defined as follows: $$ \psi_{k,r} = \begin{cases} 1 & r \equiv 0 \pmod{k} \\ 1 & r \equiv 1 \pmod{k} \\ 0 & \text{otherwise} \end{cases} $$
  • $\mathcal{B}(n,k,r)$ is the coefficient of $x^r$ in the expansion of $\left(1+x+x^2+\cdots+x^{k-1}\right)^n$.
