Rolling Dice

A few (potentially) useful results about expected values of certain die-rolling problems.

  • Suppose you roll a fair $n$-sided die (labeled $1,2,\ldots,n$) repeatedly until you get a streak of $k$, i.e. $k$ consecutive rolls in strictly increasing order (not necessarily consecutive numbers). Then the expected number of rolls is given by: $$ E(n,k) = \frac{1}{\displaystyle\sum_{r=0}^{n} \psi_{k,r}\binom{n}{r}n^{-r}} $$ Where: $$ \psi_{k,r} = \begin{cases} 1 & r \equiv 0 \pmod{k} \\ 1 & r \equiv 1 \pmod{k} \\ 0 & \text{otherwise} \end{cases} $$
